Quick Story About Me

  • Born in 2007; lived in Heidelberg, Germany from 2013 to 2018; and moved to the Seattle area since then. I currently study computer science and data science at Columbia College.
  • During my childhood in Germany, I gave a bold shot at a wide variety of interests, most notably golf, soccer, astronomy, architecture, mechatronics, business, and of course, the violin.
  • I started learning the violin in 2013 from an orchestral violinist in Heidelberg. I have wanted to be able to play like the professionals one day and bear the persuasive power of music.
  • Since my elementary school years, I've noticed an opportunity in computing. I found my hobby in updating and editing Minecraft server plugins to make them compatible with each other, so that I can organize multiplayer servers for me and my friends.
  • In my high school years, I figured that I should narrow my focus down to violin playing. In my sophomore year, I was accepted to study with Prof. Masao Kawasaki at Juilliard Pre-College. This allowed me to commit to 12-hour commutes every weekend between Seattle and New York in order to participate in a 7-hour schedule packed with music.
  • Nowadays, I am expanding my breadth into the realm of software engineering, while exploring ways in which coding can improve my daily life. So far, I have been developing my own compiler and encrypted password manager. The code for this website was written by myself.
